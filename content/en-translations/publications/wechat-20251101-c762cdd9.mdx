## Heart of the Ocean

### Color Changes of Chromium in Different Oxidation States

<Figure src="/images/uploads/wechat/wechat-20251101-c762cdd9/img-07.png" alt="Heart of the Ocean cover" />

"Heart of the Ocean" is a striking color-change demonstration. By adding hydrogen peroxide and dilute sulfuric acid to potassium chromate solution, the liquid rapidly changes from yellow to orange, then blue, and finally green. The experiment vividly shows the characteristic colors of chromium in different oxidation states.

## Chromium Colors

Hexavalent chromium appears in two common forms:

- chromate is yellow
- dichromate is orange

<Figure src="/images/uploads/wechat/wechat-20251101-c762cdd9/img-08.png" alt="Chromate ion" />
<Figure src="/images/uploads/wechat/wechat-20251101-c762cdd9/img-09.png" alt="Dichromate ion" />

Peroxochromium, often written as CrO5 in this context, appears blue:

<Figure src="/images/uploads/wechat/wechat-20251101-c762cdd9/img-10.png" alt="Peroxochromium structure" />

Trivalent chromium ions are green.

## Reaction Principle

1. Chromate reacts with hydrogen ions to form dichromate and water.

<Figure src="/images/uploads/wechat/wechat-20251101-c762cdd9/img-11.png" alt="Chromate to dichromate" />

2. Dichromate reacts with hydrogen peroxide to form peroxochromium (CrO5) and water.

<Figure src="/images/uploads/wechat/wechat-20251101-c762cdd9/img-12.png" alt="Formation of peroxochromium" />

3. Under acidic conditions, peroxochromium is reduced more quickly to Cr3+. The oxygen generated in the process is also why bubbles appear during the experiment.

<Figure src="/images/uploads/wechat/wechat-20251101-c762cdd9/img-13.png" alt="Reduction to chromium(III)" />

## Materials

- beakers
- graduated cylinder
- spatula
- dropper
- potassium chromate
- dilute sulfuric acid
- hydrogen peroxide solution

## Procedure

1. Add 0.1 g potassium chromate to beaker A, then add 150 mL water.
2. Add about one third of a dropper of hydrogen peroxide solution.
3. Add 15 mL dilute sulfuric acid to beaker B.
4. Transfer a small amount of solution from beaker A into beaker B and observe the color change.
